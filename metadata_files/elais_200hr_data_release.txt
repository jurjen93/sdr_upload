In this data release from the LOw-Frequency ARray (LOFAR), we present images covering a 2.5 × 2.5 degree field of view centred on the ELAIS-N1 deep field at 115–168 MHz.
The dataset is based on 200 hours of processed LOFAR HBA observations, including international stations, obtained from 25 individual pointings.
The data were processed using fully automated direction-dependent calibration with PILOT, producing primary-beam corrected Stokes I images with WSClean.
The field is divided into facets, representing individual patches of the full mosaic, each with their own angular resolution.
The resulting mosaics reach resolutions of 0.36″×0.45″, 0.58″×0.62″, and 1.0″×1.5″, with corresponding best sensitivities of 5.6 μJy beam⁻¹, 8.0 μJy beam⁻¹, and 14.1 μJy beam⁻¹, respectively.
We also release a source catalogue, created with PyBDSF, containing XXX radio sources, constructed by combining detections across the different resolutions.
The astrometry has an accuracy of 0.00 ± 0.029″ in right ascension and 0.00 ± 0.028″ in declination.
