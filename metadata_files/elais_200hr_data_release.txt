In this data release from the LOw-Frequency ARray (LOFAR), we present sub-arcsecond resolution images and source catalogues
covering a 2.5 × 2.5 degree field of view centred on the ELAIS-N1 deep field at 115–168 MHz.
The output data corresponds to 200 hours of processed LOFAR HBA observations, including international stations, obtained from 25 epochs.
The processing was done using fully automated direction-dependent calibration with PILOT, producing primary-beam corrected Stokes I images with WSClean.
The resulting mosaics reach resolutions of 0.36″×0.45″, 0.58″×0.62″, and 1.0″×1.5″, with corresponding best sensitivities of 5.6 μJy beam⁻¹, 8.0 μJy beam⁻¹, and 14.1 μJy beam⁻¹, respectively.
We also release a source catalogue, created with PyBDSF and based on the facet images. This contains XXX radio sources, constructed by combining detections across the different resolutions.
For science we strongly recommend using the individual facet images, representing individual patches of the full mosaics, each with their own angular resolution.
